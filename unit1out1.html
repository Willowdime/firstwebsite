1.7 Legal guidelines and constraints that impact digital projects

First let’s look into a quick overview of the 7 Principles of General Data Protection.

I : Lawfulness, Fairness and Transparency: Do not use data that you are not legally authorised to use, if you are allowed to use it in the way you said you would, and you must keep the data holder aware of the purpose, the means and the time. Let the data holder know exactly your intentions.

II : Purpose Limitation: transparency reworded basically. You can only collect and use data for the “specified, explicit and legitimate” purpose you provided.

III: Data Minimisation: Collect the minimum you need. Under the GDPR you must design an adequate policy and document why you are using the data.

IV: Accuracy: any personal data must accurate and where necessary kept up to date. You may not retain old and outdated information.

V: Storage Limitations: You need to outline the retention period for the data. You cannot hold on to it for longer than your necessary specified objectives, and it must be documented.

VI : Integrity and Confidentiality : Keep it secure. You need to handle the data in such a way that protects against unlawful processing or accidental loss, destruction or damage. And you must implement efficient anonymisation or pseudonymisation systems to protect the identity of you clients.

VII : Accountability : Record and prove compliance. Make sure you and the data holder follow through on the policies you both agreed upon. New Legislation requires a thorough documentation of all your policies that deal in data.

In addition websites are protected by copyright laws. This gives the owner of a specific art piece the right to copy and legally redistribute that art piece as they please. This means that no one else can use or sell your art piece without your documented and expressed permission. This both protects you from people copying your websites content and prevents you from doing so to others, without said permissions. Copyright is not a legal right and must be applied form, in some cases even faught over. All of the above applies to any content you create including websites.

Conclusion. A digital project will be delayed by extensive legal documentation, and further delayed by the adjustment or correction of these documents. You’ll have a huge responsibility to manage peoples info correctly, and will maybe even have financial implications in the form of employment of admin crew. The same can be said about copyrighting. The due process for verifying that you are the genuine creator of the content you wish to copyright costs time and money.
